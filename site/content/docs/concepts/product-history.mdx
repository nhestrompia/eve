---
title: Product History
description: How eve turns completed work into durable product memory.
---

Product history is the layer of knowledge that explains the meaning of implementation work.

Git can tell you that a file changed. eve is meant to tell you what shipped, which behavior changed, how the work was verified, and what future contributors should know before building on it.

## What belongs in product history

An eve snapshot should describe the completed unit of work from a product perspective:

- The title and type of change
- A concise summary
- The user-visible behavior, when applicable
- Decisions made during implementation
- Known risks and mitigations
- Validation evidence
- Artifacts such as screenshots, videos, logs, URLs, and notes
- Relationships to other snapshots

The record should be useful months later to someone who was not present when the work happened.

## What does not belong

eve is not a replacement for commit messages, code review comments, issue trackers, or long-form design docs.

Avoid using snapshots for:

- Every intermediate commit
- Raw terminal dumps without interpretation
- Private credentials or secrets
- Unverified claims
- Exhaustive implementation narration that belongs in code review

Keep the snapshot focused on the product result and the evidence for it.

## How agents benefit

Agents are good at reading structured local context. eve gives them a stable source of truth that is closer to product intent than a commit log.

Before changing a feature, an agent can inspect the related snapshots to understand prior decisions, risks, and validation expectations. After completing work, the agent can write a new snapshot that future agents and humans can trust.
